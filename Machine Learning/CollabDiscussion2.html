Discussion 2: Initial

Large Language models (LLM) are large deep learning models trained from massive amounts of data. These models are capable of producing content based on inputs in human language (Amazon Web Services, 2024). GPT-3 is a powerful example of LLM’s, it can generate content for many natural language tasks such as writing music, blog posts, automate conversational tasks and generate text summarizations (Lutkevich, N.D.).

Hutson (2021) discusses the benefits and risks of AI writers such as GPT-3, highlighting the fact that while these large generational-AI systems have lots of beneficial uses, they can also be used maliciously, make mistakes or output radicalising/harmful content.

LLM’s are trained on vast amounts of data that are normally extracted from the internet, this data is hard to regulate without affecting or biasing the model, this difficulty in regulation can mean that some of the data the model is trained on can be of discriminating nature or radical content, this reflecting in the outputs. Another Risk is the ability to generate misinformation that is hard for readers to distinguish from a human writer, and distribute quickly throughout the internet (Thompson, N.D.).

While there are risks to using AI writers and LLM’s, most of this is dependent on how it is used and it can have great benefits to people's productivity, allowing for more time spent on creativity and original thinking.
