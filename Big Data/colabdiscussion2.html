Collaborative Discussion 2

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a set of data protection rules that act as a framework for laws across the EU, it sets limits on what organisations can do and how they process personal data (Burgess, 2020).

One of the core principles stated in Art.5 GDPR is “Integrity and Confidentiality”, which states that personal data should be processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security. This includes protection against accidental loss, destruction or damage, unlawful and unauthorised processing of the data using appropriate measures (GDPR, 2018).

Since the UK left the EU, the EU GDPR does not apply in the UK and businesses operating inside of the UK need to comply with the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018), although the EU GDPR has been incorporated into UK law as the UK GDPR (ICO, 2023). The “Integrity and Confidentiality” principle from EU GDPR is referred to as the “Security Principle” by ICO.

The UK GDPR retains core principles outline in the EU GDPR, although has some modifications to wording to adapt to the domestic context (GDPR Advisor, 2023). The UK GDPR also deviates in certain areas such as data breach notification requirements, data protection officers and exemptions for certain authorities (Greenberg, 2023).
